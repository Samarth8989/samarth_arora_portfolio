How I Solved the Brand Pull Problem and Unlocked $40K+ in Monthly Revenue Through Retailer Voice

The Problem

Big brands had such strong consumer pull that they could afford to give us minimal margins—sometimes we'd actually lose money on orders. We needed higher-margin small brands, but they lacked brand recognition.

The Aha Moment

I noticed a concerning pattern: retailers who placed large orders one month would drastically reduce their orders the following month. When I started calling retailers to understand this behavior, the answer was clear: 'Customers don't ask for these small brands by name. We get stuck with inventory that moves slowly.

What I Built

Driving Brand Visibility via Retailer-Led Display Campaigns

To increase visibility and adoption for partner brands at the store level, we launched a monthly display competition for retailers with rewards ranging from ₹500–₹2,000, with a guaranteed ₹100 for participation. This resulted in a 22% increase in first orders linked to in-store displays.

Impact: 40% growth in retailer participation within first quarter

Reinforcing Repeat Behavior through Loyalty Tiers

We rolled out a structured loyalty program tied to monthly order value with tier-based slabs designed to cater to retailers across different spending bands, leading to a 12% increase in repeat order volume.

Impact: 12% increase in repeat order volume with significantly higher engagement among mid-frequency retailers

Reducing Order Cancellations through Upfront Incentives

We introduced a ₹50 confirmation fee for orders above ₹5,000 and 1% extra discount for full advance payments, resulting in a significant reduction in high-value order cancellations.

Impact: Significant reduction in high-value order cancellations

Solving Post-Booking Anxiety

Addressing uncertainty and building trust in the critical moments after ride confirmation

Problem

After the ride is confirmed, users experience uncertainty. Questions like "Why aren't they moving?", "Should I book again on another app?" create anxiety, especially for time-sensitive use cases like airport drops or meetings.

Insight

People tend to check the map obsessively right after booking. If the car doesn't move within minutes, they either call the driver, open a competing app, or cancel the ride. This behavior comes from a lack of certainty during a high-trust moment.

What I would do as a PM at Uber

Contextual Communication

Replace ETAs with reason-based updates like "Driver is waiting at a traffic light before turning toward pickup" to reduce anxiety from stationary car icons.

Driver Monitoring & Parallel Reassignment

If the assigned driver doesn't move within 2 minutes, nudge them first, then quietly search for a backup driver in parallel without creating panic.

Reliability Monetization

Allow users to "lock" a driver for ₹50 at booking. If the driver cancels within 2-3 minutes, user receives ₹100 credit for time-critical rides.

Expected Impact

These changes aim to solve not just logistical issues, but emotional friction. By addressing post-booking anxiety through transparency, backup systems, and reliability monetization, we can build deeper trust during critical moments.

Metrics to Track

Post-Match Cancellation Rate

A direct measure of user anxiety after a driver is confirmed. Should drop as transparency and control improve.

Driver Call Frequency

Indicates trust gaps. Should decrease over time as users become familiar with clearer, in-app updates.

Ride Lock Adoption Rate

Reflects user demand for reliability during high-stakes situations.

Support Ticket Volume

Lower inbound support = better communication, less confusion, and smoother UX.

Building LinkedIn's Feed Relevance

Transforming the professional feed from clickbait-driven to career-focused through intelligent content curation and user preferences

Problem

LinkedIn is meant to help professionals discover content that supports career growth, but the feed is filled with clickbaity posts that feel generic. Users scroll through content that doesn't match their interests, role, or goals, making the platform feel less useful over time.

What I would do as a PM at LinkedIn

Tag-Based Content Preferences

Allow users to select specific topics, skills, and industry areas they want to see more of. Use these preferences to weight content in the algorithm.

Role-Specific Content Surfacing

Leverage job title and industry data to surface content that's specifically relevant to the user's current role and career stage.

Anti-Viral Content Detection

Implement systems to detect when similar content templates are being overused. Reduce distribution of copycat posts and reward original content.

Content Quality Scoring

Develop quality metrics based on engagement depth, save rates, and time spent reading. Prioritize substantive content over viral but shallow posts.

Expected Impact

By focusing on relevance over virality, LinkedIn can become a more valuable professional tool. Users will spend more meaningful time on the platform and engage with content that actually helps their careers.

Success Metrics

Content Relevance Score

User surveys on how relevant their feed content feels to their professional goals and interests.

Engagement Quality

Ratio of meaningful comments to surface-level reactions. Higher quality engagement indicates more valuable content.

Time Well Spent

User-reported satisfaction with time spent on LinkedIn. Are users learning and growing from their feed experience?

Content Diversity

Reduction in duplicate or template-based content. Increase in original, industry-specific insights.
